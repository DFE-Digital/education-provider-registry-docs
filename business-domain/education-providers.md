## Business Definition

An education provider is an individual school, academy, college, centre or other organisation that provides education or education-related services.

It is the provider being described in its own right. It has a name, location, provider kind, operating status, age range or phase where applicable, and other facts about the provision it offers.

Education providers include:

- Maintained schools.
- Academies.
- Free schools.
- Independent schools.
- Special schools.
- Pupil referral units and alternative provision.
- Further education and sixth-form providers.
- Higher education providers.
- Children's centres.
- Online providers.
- British schools overseas.
- Other specialist or government-funded providers.

The core business question is:

```text
Which individual school, academy, college, centre or provider is this?
```

That is different from:

```text
Which trust, federation, sponsor or organisation is connected to it?
Who governs it?
```

## Why Education Providers Matter

Education providers are the places or services through which education, childcare, alternative provision, specialist provision or post-16 learning is delivered.

People need to understand providers because they answer practical questions such as:

- What is the provider called?
- What kind of provider is it?
- Where is it located?
- Is it open, closed, proposed to open or proposed to close?
- What age range or phase does it serve?
- Does it provide mainstream, special, alternative, post-16, online or early-years provision?
- Which organisation is responsible for it?
- Which governance body oversees it?
- Which identifiers refer to it?
- Which public information should be available about it?

The provider is therefore the anchor for many other business facts: identity, location, status, provision, accountability, governance and relationships.

## Provider, Organisation And Governance

Three concepts should be kept separate:

| Concept | Business Question | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Education provider | Which individual school, academy, college, centre or provider is this? | Oakfield Academy. |
| Provider organisation | Which wider trust, federation, sponsor, group or collaboration is connected to it? | Oakfield Learning Trust. |
| Governance | Who is responsible for overseeing the provider or organisation? | Trust board, governing body, trustees, governors. |

Example:

```text
Provider:
Oakfield Academy

Provider kind:
Academy converter

Provider organisation:
Oakfield Learning Trust

Governance:
Trust board and trustees
```

These are related facts, but they are not the same fact.

## Provider Identity

Provider identity is the set of facts that distinguish one provider from another.

For business purposes, provider identity can include:

- Provider name.
- Unique provider identifiers.
- Local authority context where relevant.
- Local provider number where relevant.
- UK Provider Reference Number where relevant.
- Previous identifiers or predecessor context.
- Address and postcode.
- Public contact details.

Some identifiers are national. Some are meaningful only within a local authority or sector. Some providers may have sector-specific identifiers that do not apply to every provider kind.

In business terms:

```text
The provider is the real-world school, academy, college, centre or service.
The identifier is a way of referring to it.
```

## Provider Kind

Provider kind describes what sort of education provider something is.

This matters because different provider kinds have different legal forms, accountability routes, governance arrangements, public information, age ranges and provision characteristics.

Provider kind is not the same as provider organisation.

For example:

| Provider | Provider kind | Provider organisation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| River Primary Academy | Academy converter | River Learning Trust. |
| North Street Primary School | Community school | Local authority maintained school context. |
| West Town Free School | Free school | West Town Learning Trust. |
| South Road Children's Centre | Children's centre | Local children's-centre group or local authority context. |

In business terms:

```text
Provider kind says what the provider is.
Provider organisation says what wider organisation or relationship sits around it.
```

## Maintained Schools

Maintained schools are state-funded schools funded through the local authority.

GOV.UK describes several maintained school categories, including:

- Community schools.
- Foundation schools.
- Voluntary aided schools.
- Voluntary controlled schools.

Maintained schools have a governing body. They are not academy trusts and they are not operated through a funding agreement with the Secretary of State in the same way as academies.

In business terms:

```text
A maintained school is an individual school.
It has a local authority context.
It has maintained-school governance.
```

## Community Schools

A community school is a maintained school where the local authority has a strong role.

Community schools are funded through the local authority. The local authority generally employs the staff, owns the land and buildings, and is responsible for admissions.

In business terms:

```text
A community school is a locally maintained school.
```

## Foundation And Voluntary Schools

Foundation schools, voluntary aided schools and voluntary controlled schools are maintained schools with additional foundation, trust or voluntary-body context.

The precise responsibilities differ by category. For example, voluntary aided schools often have stronger governing-body and foundation responsibilities than voluntary controlled schools.

In business terms:

```text
These are maintained schools, but their ownership, employment, admissions or foundation arrangements may differ.
```

## Academies

An academy is a state-funded school operated by an academy trust.

Academies are independent of local authority control and operate under academy funding agreements. The academy trust is the legal organisation accountable for the academy.

Academy kinds include:

- Academy converter.
- Academy sponsor led.
- Academy special converter.
- Academy special sponsor led.
- Academy alternative provision converter.
- Academy alternative provision sponsor led.
- Academy 16 to 19 converter.
- Academy 16 to 19 sponsor led.
- Academy secure 16 to 19.

In business terms:

```text
The academy is the provider.
The academy trust is the legal organisation that operates it.
```

## Free Schools, UTCs And Studio Schools

Free schools are state-funded schools created through the free-school route. They are academies in legal and governance terms because they are operated by academy trusts.

Related provider kinds include:

- Free school.
- Free school special.
- Free school alternative provision.
- Free school 16 to 19.
- University technical college.
- Studio school.

University technical colleges and studio schools have distinctive education models, but they sit within the academy trust landscape.

In business terms:

```text
These are provider kinds.
The academy trust is the operating organisation.
```

## Special Schools And Specialist Provision

Special schools and specialist providers serve children and young people with special educational needs, disabilities or other specialist requirements.

Provider kinds include:

- Community special school.
- Foundation special school.
- Non-maintained special school.
- Other independent special school.
- Academy special converter.
- Academy special sponsor led.
- Free school special.
- Special post-16 institution.

Specialist provision may also exist within mainstream schools through resourced provision, SEN units or special classes. That does not make the mainstream school a special school. It means the provider has a specialist provision characteristic.

In business terms:

```text
Special-school provider kind and specialist provision within another school are related, but different.
```

## Pupil Referral Units And Alternative Provision

A pupil referral unit is a local-authority form of alternative provision for children who are unable to attend mainstream school.

Alternative provision may also be provided through academy or free-school routes, such as academy alternative provision or free school alternative provision.

In business terms:

```text
Alternative provision describes the purpose of the provision.
The provider kind describes the legal or operating form through which that provision is delivered.
```

## Independent Schools

Independent schools are schools that are not maintained by a local authority and are not academies.

Provider kinds include:

- Other independent school.
- Other independent special school.
- City technology college.

Independent schools may have a proprietor rather than an academy trust or maintained-school governing body. Their inspection and public information arrangements can differ from maintained schools and academies.

In business terms:

```text
An independent school is a provider outside the maintained-school and academy-trust models.
```

## Colleges And Post-16 Providers

Post-16 and college-style providers include:

- Further education providers.
- Sixth-form centres.
- Special post-16 institutions.

These providers are not all schools. Their governance, funding, admissions and public information requirements can differ from school-based provision.

In business terms:

```text
College and post-16 providers need their own business treatment.
They should not be forced into school-only assumptions.
```

## Higher Education Providers

Higher education providers include universities and other higher education institutions.

They have different regulatory, funding and governance contexts from schools, academies and further education colleges.

In business terms:

```text
Higher education providers are part of the wider education landscape, but they are not school providers.
```

## Children's Centres

Children's centres provide early childhood services and family support.

Provider kinds include:

- Children's centre.
- Children's centre linked site.

A children's centre linked site is connected to a wider children's-centre arrangement and may offer services on behalf of another centre.

In business terms:

```text
Children's centres are providers of early childhood and family services.
They should not be treated as schools.
```

## Online Providers

Online providers deliver education through an online model.

The online education accreditation scheme provides a quality assurance route for online education providers. Online provider information can include accreditation status, accreditation dates and quality assurance body information.

In business terms:

```text
An online provider is a provider kind with accreditation and quality-assurance facts that do not apply to most school types.
```

## British Schools Overseas And Offshore Provision

Some providers sit outside the normal England local-authority geography.

These include:

- British schools overseas.
- Offshore schools.
- Service children's education.

These providers may offer British-style education or serve particular overseas communities, but their location and accountability context differs from ordinary schools in England.

In business terms:

```text
Overseas and offshore providers need location and accountability context that ordinary England-based schools do not.
```

## Other Specialist Provider Kinds

Some provider kinds exist for specialist or uncommon cases.

Examples include:

- Secure units.
- Institution funded by other government department.
- Miscellaneous provider records.
- Welsh provider records.

These categories should be handled carefully. They may not follow the usual school, academy, college or children's-centre patterns.

In business terms:

```text
Specialist provider kinds should not be forced into mainstream-school assumptions.
```

## Provider Lifecycle

Providers change over time.

A provider may be:

- Open.
- Closed.
- Proposed to open.
- Open but proposed to close.

Lifecycle information can include:

- Open date.
- Close date.
- Reason opened.
- Reason closed.
- Successor or predecessor context.

Lifecycle is about the operating state of the real-world provider. It is not the same thing as whether the provider's record is current or whether information about it has recently been confirmed.

In business terms:

```text
Provider lifecycle describes the life of the provider.
Record currency describes the freshness of information about it.
```

## Provider Information

For business purposes, it may be useful to know:

- Provider name.
- Provider kind.
- Operating status.
- Address and contact details.
- Age range or education phase.
- Admissions arrangement where relevant.
- Gender of entry where relevant.
- Boarding provision where relevant.
- Nursery, sixth-form or special-class provision where relevant.
- SEN and resourced provision where relevant.
- Religious character, ethos or diocesan context where relevant.
- Capacity and pupil measures where relevant.
- Inspection, accreditation or quality assurance links where relevant.
- Provider organisation relationships.
- Governance arrangements.

This does not mean every provider has every item of information. Different provider kinds need different facts.

## Business Rules And Boundaries

The following boundaries are important:

- An education provider is the individual school, academy, college, centre or provider being described.
- A provider organisation is the wider trust, federation, sponsor, group or collaboration connected to one or more providers.
- Governance describes who oversees the provider or provider organisation.
- Provider kind should not be confused with provider organisation.
- A school is not always the right word for every provider.
- An academy is a provider operated by an academy trust.
- An academy trust is not the academy itself.
- A free school, UTC or studio school is a provider kind, even though it is operated through an academy trust.
- A federation is a shared maintained-school governance arrangement, not a provider replacing the member schools.
- Children's centres, online providers, colleges, higher education providers and overseas providers need their own language.
- Not every fact applies to every provider kind.

## Business Implication

Education providers should be understood as the individual schools, academies, colleges, centres and services that deliver education or education-related provision.

That means:

- Do not use "school" as a universal label for every provider.
- Do not confuse the provider with the trust, federation, sponsor or organisation around it.
- Do not confuse provider kind with governance.
- Keep provider identity, provider kind, lifecycle, provision, organisation relationships and governance distinct.
- Allow different provider kinds to have different information needs.

This gives a clearer way to describe the education landscape: individual providers, the organisations around them, and the governance arrangements that hold them to account.
