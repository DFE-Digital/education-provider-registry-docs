## Business Definition

Governance describes how an education provider, school, academy trust, federation or college is overseen, held to account and directed.

It is about the people and bodies responsible for strategic oversight, compliance, stewardship and accountability. It is not the same as day-to-day management, teaching, inspection, funding or provider classification.

Governance includes:

- The accountable body, such as a governing body or trust board.
- The people who hold governance roles, such as governors, trustees and members.
- The governance structure around one or more providers.
- Appointment, term and role information.
- Public accountability information.
- Legal or policy documents that define governance responsibilities.

The core business question is:

```text
Who is responsible for governing this provider, trust, federation or group?
```

That is different from:

```text
Who manages the provider day to day?
What type of provider is this?
Which provider organisation is it linked to?
```

Governance can sit at different levels. Some governance sits directly around a maintained school. Some sits around a federation of maintained schools. Some sits at academy trust level, with local governance delegated to committees or local governing bodies.

## Why Governance Matters

Governance matters because education providers are publicly accountable organisations.

Good governance explains:

- Who is accountable for strategic decisions.
- Who oversees educational standards and financial control.
- Who is appointed to governance roles.
- How long people serve in those roles.
- Which body appointed them.
- Whether governance is local to one provider or shared across several.
- Which information must be published for transparency.
- Which information is collected but not published.

Without governance information, it is difficult to answer questions such as:

- Who chairs the governing body?
- Which trustees are responsible for an academy trust?
- Which members sit above the trust board?
- Does this academy have a local governing body?
- Is this school part of a federation with a shared governing body?
- Who is the accounting officer?
- Who is the chief financial officer?
- Has a governor, trustee or member recently stepped down?
- Which people govern in more than one school, academy or trust?

Governance therefore connects people, roles, organisations, appointments, dates and accountability structures.

## Governance Is Not Universal Across All Provider Types

The strongest national sources for governor information focus on maintained schools, academies, academy trusts and multi-academy trusts.

The national database of governors was introduced for those involved in the governance of:

- Maintained schools.
- Academies.
- Multi-academy trusts.

It records roles such as:

- Maintained school governors.
- Academy trustees.
- Academy members.
- Local governors.

This does not mean no other education provider has governance. Further education colleges, higher education providers, independent schools and children's centres may have governance or ownership arrangements of their own.

It does mean that school and academy governance should not be assumed to apply in exactly the same way to every provider type.

In business terms:

```text
Governance is essential for maintained schools, academies and academy trusts.
Other provider types need their own governance evidence and terminology.
```

## Maintained School Governance

A maintained school is funded through a local authority and has a governing body.

GOV.UK describes the governing body as the accountable body for the school. Governors sit on the governing body and are responsible for governance rather than day-to-day management.

Maintained school governance can include:

- Governors.
- A chair of governors.
- Committees.
- A governance professional or clerk.
- Appointment and term information.
- Appointing-body information.

Maintained school categories include community schools, foundation schools, voluntary schools, maintained nursery schools and maintained special schools.

The maintained schools governance guide says it does not apply directly to pupil referral units, sixth-form colleges or general further education colleges, although they may find its principles helpful. That is an important business caveat: not every publicly funded provider should automatically be treated as having the same maintained-school governance model.

In business terms:

```text
The maintained school is the provider.
The governing body is the accountable governance body.
Governors are people appointed to that governing body.
```

## Federated Maintained Schools

A federation is a statutory governance arrangement for maintained schools.

GOV.UK federation guidance defines a federation as two or more maintained schools operating under the governance of a single governing body. The guidance applies to maintained schools and maintained nursery schools.

In a federation:

- The schools remain separate schools.
- The federation has one shared governing body.
- The governing body governs the federated schools.
- The federation does not create a single merged school.

This is different from an academy trust.

In business terms:

```text
A federation is shared maintained-school governance.
It is not an academy trust.
It is not a new school replacing the member schools.
```

Example:

```text
Federation:
North and South Primary Federation

Member schools:
- North Street Primary School
- South Road Primary School

The schools remain separate maintained schools.
The federation represents their shared governing body.
```

## Academy Trust Governance

An academy trust is the legal organisation responsible for one or more academies.

GOV.UK defines an academy trust as an independent charitable company limited by guarantee with a funding agreement with the Secretary of State for Education.

The academy trust board is the accountable body for the trust. Academy trustees sit on the board and are also company directors and charity trustees.

Academy trust governance can include:

- Members.
- Academy trustees.
- A chair of trustees.
- A board of trustees.
- An accounting officer.
- A chief financial officer.
- Governance professionals.
- Committees.
- Local committees or local governing bodies.

The Academy Trust Handbook provides the financial management and control framework for academy trusts. Academy trusts must comply with the handbook as a condition of their funding agreement.

In business terms:

```text
The academy is the provider.
The academy trust is the legal organisation accountable for it.
The trust board governs the trust.
The trustees sit on the trust board.
```

## Multi-Academy Trusts

A multi-academy trust, or MAT, is an academy trust responsible for more than one academy.

GOV.UK describes a MAT as a single legal entity. It is accountable for all academies in the trust.

This is important because a MAT is not a loose federation of separate academy companies. It is one legal trust organisation. The individual academies remain separate schools or providers, but they sit inside the same accountable trust.

In business terms:

```text
A MAT is one academy trust.
It operates multiple academies.
The trust board is accountable for the trust as a whole.
```

Example:

```text
Multi-academy trust:
River Learning Trust

Academies in the trust:
- River Primary Academy
- River Secondary Academy
- River Studio School

Each academy is a provider.
The MAT is the legal trust organisation around them.
```

## Single-Academy Trusts

A single-academy trust, or SAT, is an academy trust responsible for one academy.

It is still a legal trust organisation. It is still governed by members and trustees. It happens to operate one academy rather than several.

In business terms:

```text
A SAT is the trust.
The academy is the provider.
The SAT and the academy should not be treated as the same business thing.
```

## Members

Members sit above the academy trust board.

They are responsible for key constitutional matters, such as appointing some trustees and holding the board to account for the trust's governance arrangements.

Members are not the same as trustees. They are part of the academy trust's governance structure, but they do not carry out the same role as the board.

In business terms:

```text
Members are guardians of the trust's governance structure.
Trustees are responsible for governing the trust.
```

## Trustees

Academy trustees sit on the academy trust board.

They are responsible for the trust's strategy, accountability and compliance. They are also company directors and charity trustees.

Trustees can be associated with:

- A multi-academy trust.
- A single-academy trust.
- A trust that operates free schools, studio schools, UTCs or other academy providers.

The chair of trustees leads the trust board.

In business terms:

```text
Trustees govern the academy trust.
They do not govern only one academy unless the trust itself operates only one academy.
```

## Local Governing Bodies And Local Committees

In a multi-academy trust, local governance can be delegated to a local committee or local governing body.

GOV.UK academy trust guidance describes local committees as committees of the board. The trust remains the legal entity and the board remains accountable for the academies in the trust.

This matters because local governors are not the same as trustees. A local governing body can provide local oversight, but it does not replace the trust board.

In business terms:

```text
The trust board is accountable for the trust.
Local governance is delegated by the trust board.
Local governors support governance at academy or local-cluster level.
```

## Accounting Officers And Chief Financial Officers

Academy trusts have specific senior financial and accountability roles.

The accounting officer is normally the senior executive leader of the academy trust. The role is personally responsible to Parliament for regularity, propriety and value for money.

The chief financial officer is responsible for the trust's financial operations and financial control.

These are not generic school-staff labels. They are governance and accountability roles associated with academy trusts.

In business terms:

```text
The accounting officer and chief financial officer are trust-level accountability roles.
```

## Governance Professionals

Governance professionals support good governance.

They may be described as governance professionals or clerks, depending on the context. They help governing bodies, trust boards and committees operate effectively.

Governance professional roles can be associated with:

- Maintained schools.
- Federations.
- Single-academy trusts.
- Multi-academy trusts.
- Academy local governance.

In business terms:

```text
A governance professional supports the governance body.
The role is not simply an administrative contact.
```

## Appointments, Terms And Appointing Bodies

Governance is not just a list of people. A person holds a role through an appointment.

An appointment can have:

- A governance role.
- An appointing body.
- A start date.
- A term of office.
- A term-end date.
- A stepped-down date.
- A current or historic status.

The appointing body matters because it explains how the person came to hold the governance role. In maintained schools, publication guidance refers to the appointing body in accordance with the governing body's instrument of government. In academy trusts, appointment powers can be defined by articles of association and other trust documents.

In business terms:

```text
The person is not the appointment.
The role is not the appointment.
The appointment links a person to a governance role for a period of time.
```

## Public Governance Information

Governance has a transparency purpose.

GOV.UK publication guidance says maintained schools should publish information about their governing body and committees. It also says they should publish information such as governor names, categories, appointing bodies, terms of office, attendance and interests.

GOV.UK publication guidance for academies and further education colleges says academy trusts must publish governance arrangements, member and trustee names, relevant interests, articles of association, funding agreements and supplemental funding agreements.

The national database of governors source says public governance data includes:

- Full name, including title.
- Appointing body.
- Date of appointment.
- Date term of office ends or ended, where it ended within the last year.
- Role category.

This explains why governance information often includes both current people and people who have recently left.

In business terms:

```text
Governance information is not only operational data.
Some of it exists because public accountability requires it to be published.
```

## Restricted Governance Information

Not all governance information is public.

The national database of governors source says some collected information is not published, including:

- Postcode.
- Date of birth.
- Previous names.
- Direct chair email.

This distinction is important. Public governance information and restricted governance information have different purposes.

In business terms:

```text
Some governance data supports public transparency.
Some governance data supports identification, contact or assurance.
The two should not be confused.
```

## Further Education Governance

Further education colleges have governance expectations, but they are not the same as maintained-school governing bodies or academy trust boards.

GOV.UK publication guidance for academies and further education colleges says FE colleges should publish information about their governing body structure and responsibilities, committee details, names of the chair and governors, recruitment information, and instruments and articles of government.

In business terms:

```text
FE governance exists.
It should not be assumed to follow the same role structure as school and academy governance.
```

## Church And Religious-Character Governance

Some schools and academies have religious character.

GOV.UK church academy model documents show that church academy trusts can have particular governance arrangements. Appointment rights and governance responsibilities may involve religious authorities or foundation bodies.

This matters most for appointing-body information and role categories.

In business terms:

```text
Religious-character governance may need additional appointing-body context.
```

## Governance And Provider Organisations

Governance and provider organisations are related, but they are not the same concept.

A provider organisation is the organisation or governance structure around one or more providers. Governance describes who is responsible for governing that organisation, structure or provider.

For example:

| Provider or organisation concept | Governance concept |
| --- | --- |
| Community school | Governing body, governors, chair of governors. |
| Federation | Shared governing body across the federated schools. |
| Single-academy trust | Members, trustees, chair of trustees, accounting officer, chief financial officer. |
| Multi-academy trust | Trust board, members, trustees and delegated local governance. |
| Academy within a MAT | Local governing body or local committee, if the trust has one. |

In business terms:

```text
The provider organisation is the organisation or arrangement.
Governance is the accountability structure around it.
```

## Business Rules And Boundaries

The following boundaries are important:

- Governance is not the same as provider type.
- Governance is not the same as provider-organisation membership or relationship.
- A maintained school has a governing body.
- A federation is a shared governing body across maintained schools.
- A MAT is one legal trust entity accountable for multiple academies.
- A SAT is one legal trust entity accountable for one academy.
- Academy members are not the same as academy trustees.
- Trustees are not the same as local governors.
- Local governance in a MAT is delegated from the trust board.
- Accounting officer and chief financial officer are academy trust accountability roles.
- Governance professionals support governance bodies and should not be treated as ordinary provider contacts.
- Appointment dates, term dates and appointing bodies are part of the governance appointment.
- Public governance information is not the whole governance record.
- Further education governance should not be assumed to use the same role structure as school and academy governance.
- Children's centre governance is not defined by the main school and academy governance sources listed above.

## Plain-English Glossary

| Term | Plain-English Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Governance | The oversight and accountability structure around a provider, school, trust, federation or college. |
| Governing body | The accountable governance body for a maintained school or federation. |
| Governor | A person appointed to a maintained-school governing body. |
| Chair of governors | The person who chairs a maintained-school governing body. |
| Federation | Two or more maintained schools under one governing body. |
| Academy trust | The legal organisation accountable for one or more academies. |
| Trust board | The board responsible for governing an academy trust. |
| Member | A person or body that sits above the academy trust board and helps protect the trust's governance arrangements. |
| Trustee | A person on the academy trust board; also a company director and charity trustee. |
| Chair of trustees | The person who chairs the academy trust board. |
| Local governing body | A local governance body or committee used by an academy trust where governance is delegated locally. |
| Local governor | A person appointed to local governance in an academy trust. |
| Accounting officer | The academy trust senior executive leader with personal accountability for regularity, propriety and value for money. |
| Chief financial officer | The person responsible for academy trust financial operations and control. |
| Governance professional | A person who supports the operation and effectiveness of governance. |
| Appointing body | The body or authority responsible for appointing a person to a governance role. |
| Term of office | The period for which a person is appointed to a governance role. |
| Historic governance role | A governance role that has ended but remains relevant for recent-history or publication purposes. |

## Business Implication

Governance should be understood as a set of accountable bodies, people, appointments and role relationships.

That means:

- Do not treat governance as a simple list of names.
- Do not treat every education provider as having the same governance structure.
- Keep maintained-school governance, academy trust governance, federation governance and FE governance distinct.
- Keep members, trustees, governors, local governors and governance professionals distinct.
- Keep the person, the role and the appointment period distinct.
- Keep public governance information separate from restricted governance information.
- Treat provider organisations and governance as related but separate business concepts.

This gives a clearer way to describe how education providers are overseen: the provider or organisation being governed, the body that governs it, the people appointed to governance roles, and the accountability rules around those appointments.
